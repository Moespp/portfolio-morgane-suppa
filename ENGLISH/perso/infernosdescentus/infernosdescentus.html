INFERNO'S DESCENT

#Trpg #3D #DnD #ProofOfConcept

Inferno's Descent is a Proof Of Concept for a T-RPG game based on a tabletop RPG campaign inspired by the book Dungeons & Dragons: Descent into Avernus.

For this project, I worked on Game & Level Design, Narrative Design, as well as Level Art.

Engine: Unreal Engine 5.2

Tools: Miro, Discord, Procreate

Gameplay

The game offers a rich exploration experience, with vast areas to discover and numerous secrets to unveil. Throughout their adventure, players will face a variety of enemies. They must navigate skillfully through complex environments, overcoming obstacles to progress in the game.

Exploration and problem-solving are essential for progression. Here, characters find themselves trapped in a strange hangar. By exploring, players can uncover information about the place and discover the objective to accomplish. They might find notes left by previous occupants, hidden mechanisms, or useful items to unlock doors or disable traps.
The camera allows players to switch from an overhead view reminiscent of classic board games to an immersive view. This transition between perspectives gives players control over their visual experience. Inspired by BG3's camera, this feature enhances appreciation for the game's strategy and details. The overhead view is ideal for planning actions and navigation, while the close-up view allows players to appreciate details.

We created a proof of concept for a game that incorporates tabletop role-playing game principles into a video game format. The goal was to stay true to the roots of pen-and-paper RPGs. We worked on an immersive camera consistent with the genre, as well as integrating a dice rolling mechanic to determine outcomes of actions in the game. We transformed an existing pen-and-paper RPG campaign into a simple playable demo.
